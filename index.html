Curricular Practical Training (CPT)

CPT provides the opportunity for international students to gain professional training and to work in paid internship positions. The students have the chance to apply for an authorized job and work full-time or part-time with the option of salary or just gain work experience in their area of interest, this will be up to the employer. The training must relate directly to the student’s major area of study and the authorization is for one specific employer and for a specific period of time. One year of full-time CPT eliminates a student’s eligibility for OPT.

The DSO Office is the main point of contact for issues related to your SEVIS record (Form I-20). Designated School Officials serve as liaisons between international students, the school, and the U.S. government. DSOs issue necessary immigration forms, and guide students through the process of studying in the U.S. The DSO Office will assist you to update your student record and help you maintain your student status by providing advice or helping you fill out important forms.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is "status"?

As an F-1 student, "status" refers to the requirements to stay in compliance with the Student and Visitor Exchange Program (SEVP). Violations of this "status" may result in the removal from the SEVP. Students are expected to follow all rules and regulations set forth by the SEVP and the U.S. Department of State.

How do I maintain "status"?

Full Course of Study:

F-1 students are expected to enroll in a full-course of study and must make normal progress towards completion of their program. A full-course of study is defined as:

Undergraduates:

  • 12 Credit Hours

Graduates:

  • 9 Credit Hours

PhD Candidates:

  • 6 Credit Hours

Only ONE 3-credit course may be taken online per semester to count towards a full-course of study . If a student is in their final semester with only one course remaining, it must be taken in person. Further, international students must be physically present in the United States for any online classes to be counted towards a full-course of study requirement.

Under certain circumstances, students may be eligible for a Reduced Course Load or RCL . This must be approved by the DSO PRIOR to a student dropping classes or taking less than a full time course load.

Employment Regulations

Students on an F-1 visa are eligible to work on-campus up to 20 hours per week while school is in session, and up to full-time while on oschool breaks or during annual vacation . It is the student's responsibility to carefully monitor the number of hours they are working each week, as a violation of working hours can result in a termination of the student's status.

International Student Employment

Student Employment

Students on an F-1 visa are eligible to work on-campus up to 20 hours per week while school is in session, and up to full-time while on school breaks or during annual vacation. It is the student's responsibility to carefully monitor the number of hours they are working each week, as a violation of working hours can result in a termination of the student's status.

On-Campus Employment

On-Campus employment is categorized as work that is done on school premises for Carolina University or for an approved vendor. Once approved for work-study, international students can apply for a Social Security Card for work purposes. Students must follow all employment rules and regulations to avoid any violations of F-1 status for unauthorized employment.

International Students Must Adhere to the Following Regulations:

  • Students may only work up to 20 hours per week while school is in session
  • Students may only work up to full-time per week while on school breaks or annual vacation
  • Students must report any work to their DSO and work-study office and must apply for a Social Security Number (if eligible)
